Common Words Across SOTU Speeches: 1980-2014


Size: Frequency of Word,



Color/Location: Distribution Across Democratic vs. Republican Presidents' SOTU Speeches

Background:

The Washington Post created an article around words used in various SOTUs. (See post here.) While the graphic works in many ways, it is hard to compare the frequency across words and party in many cases.

This blog post is an attempt to show party discrepancies across word choices. The most apparent result (something the Washington Post missed by lumping together) is "America" (R) vs. "Americans" (D). There are also a few anomolies -- "help" sits on the Republican side while "economy" rests with Democratic presidents. In addition, Republicans tend to repeat a lot of the same words while Democrats have a larger vocabulary incorporated into their speeches.
